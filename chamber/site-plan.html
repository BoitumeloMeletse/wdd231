Mountain Trail Explorers

Site Plan Document

Site Name

Mountain Trail Explorers

This name was selected because it clearly communicates the focus of the website - exploring mountain trails. The name evokes a sense of adventure and discovery while being descriptive enough to immediately convey the site's purpose to visitors. The word "Explorers" suggests a community aspect, implying that the site is for people who actively seek out and enjoy mountain trails.

Optional domain availability: mountaintrailexplorers.com or trail-explorers.org

Site Purpose

The Mountain Trail Explorers website will serve as a comprehensive resource for hiking and outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore mountain trails. The site will provide:

  • A searchable database of mountain trails with detailed information including difficulty ratings, elevation profiles, distance, estimated completion time, and trail features
  • Interactive trail maps with markers for key points of interest, rest areas, water sources, and scenic viewpoints
  • Real-time weather updates and forecasts for trail areas to help hikers plan their trips safely
  • User-generated reviews, photos, and current trail condition reports
  • Educational content for hikers of all experience levels, including safety tips, gear recommendations, and environmental conservation practices
  • A community forum where hikers can connect, organize group hikes, and share experiences

The primary goal is to make mountain hiking more accessible and safer by providing accurate, up-to-date information in an easy-to-use format, while fostering a community of responsible outdoor enthusiasts.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: "I'm a beginner hiker looking for trails suitable for my experience level. What are some easy to moderate trails near me that offer good views but aren't too challenging?"

Scenario 2: "I'm planning a day hike this weekend, but I'm concerned about the weather. What are the current trail conditions and weather forecast for the Mount Wilson trail, and are there any reported hazards I should be aware of?"

Scenario 3: "I'm an experienced hiker looking for challenging trails with significant elevation gain. Which trails in the Rocky Mountains offer the most rewarding summit views and what technical gear might I need?"

Scenario 4: "I'm hiking with my dog next weekend. Which trails are pet-friendly and have water sources along the route where my dog can drink?"

Color Schema

The color scheme is inspired by nature and the outdoors, creating a fresh, trustworthy feel that reflects the mountain hiking experience.

#2B6CB0 Primary Blue
#48BB78 Secondary Green
#F6AD55 Accent Orange
#1A365D Dark Blue
#EBF8FF Light Blue

Color Application

  • Primary Blue (#2B6CB0): Main header background, primary buttons, and key interactive elements. This color represents the sky and water elements found in mountain environments.
  • Secondary Green (#48BB78): Section headings underlines, success messages, and elements related to trail information. This color represents the forests and vegetation of mountain trails.
  • Accent Orange (#F6AD55): Call-to-action elements, highlighted information, and warning indicators. This warm color provides contrast and draws attention to important elements.
  • Dark Blue (#1A365D): Footer background, headings, and text links. This deeper blue provides strong contrast for important text elements.
  • Light Blue (#EBF8FF): Page background, card backgrounds, and secondary information panels. This subtle blue creates a clean, airy feel reminiscent of mountain atmospheres.
  • Text Color (#2D3748): Main body text for optimal readability against light backgrounds.
